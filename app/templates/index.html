        Under the hood

        How a winner gets picked

        The plots below come straight out of the trained model, scored on 544 games it never touched while learning. Click any chart to blow it up.

        01

        XGBoost

        It grows decision trees one after another. Each new tree studies where the last batch went wrong and patches those specific misses. Stack a few hundred and you get the model that usually wins on spreadsheet-style data like this.

        02

        Recent form, not season totals

        Every team stat is its average over the last five games, so a team that caught fire in November looks hot instead of average. The game being predicted never sneaks into its own five-game window, which is how you avoid feeding the model the answer.

        03

        Football rules, baked in

        The model is told a bigger yardage or scoring edge can only ever help the team that holds it. That guardrail stops it from learning backwards quirks from small samples, and it nudged accuracy up at the same time.

        04

        SHAP shows its work

        For a single game, SHAP measures how far each stat pushed the pick and in which direction. That math is exactly what fills the "what drove the model's pick" line above, so the number always comes with a reason.
